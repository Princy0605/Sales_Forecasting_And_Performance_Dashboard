Project Name: Sales Forecasting & Performance Dashboard

## Description:
This project analyzes historical sales data to provide actionable business insights. The analysis covers time-based trends, product performance, and customer behavior to help stakeholders make data-driven decisions.

## Dataset:
The dataset used is 'train.csv', containing sales records with columns including Order Date, Customer, Product, Category, Region, Sales etc.

## Key Analyses Performed:

1. Monthly Sales Trend:
   - Aggregated monthly sales to identify seasonal patterns and peak months.
   - Visualized trends using line charts to show revenue growth over time.

2. Yearly Sales Analysis:
   - Calculated total revenue per year.
   - Highlighted year-over-year growth and trends.

3. Quarterly Analysis:
   - Grouped sales by quarters to observe business cycles.
   - Identified high-performing quarters for revenue optimization.

4. Top Categories & Sub-Categories:
   - Ranked product categories and sub-categories by total sales.
   - Identified key revenue-driving products and categories.

5. Top 5 Products:
   - Highlighted the top 5 revenue-generating products.
   - Visualized sales contributions using horizontal bar charts for readability.

6. Customer Insights:
   - Identified the top 10 customers contributing most to revenue.
   - Showed the impact of key customers on overall sales performance.

7. Regional Analysis:
   - Grouped sales by Region, State, Country, and City.
   - Identified top-performing areas and revenue contribution by location.


## Tools & Libraries Used:
- Python
- Pandas & NumPy for data manipulation
- Matplotlib & Seaborn for visualization

## Key Insights:
- Highest Revenue was generated in the End of the year(November)
- Technology and Furniture categories generate the majority of revenue.
- Q4 and certain months consistently show peak sales, indicating seasonality.
- A small group of top customers contributes a significant portion of total revenue.
- Top products significantly influence overall revenue and should be prioritized for marketing and inventory planning.
